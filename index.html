Saturnia pavonia

Saturnia pavonia, commonly known as the Emperor Moth, is a striking species of silk moth native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It’s the only member of the Saturniidae family found in the British Isles, and it’s a visual marvel—especially the males.

Key Features are Wingspan of Males is 60 mm, and Females is 80 mm.

Antennae: Males sport feathery antennae to detect female pheromones. Females have thinner, less ornate antennae.

Coloration: Males have reddish-brown forewings with bold black and orange eyespots and orange hindwings. Females are larger, more muted in grey and white tones, but still carry the signature eyespots on all wings.
